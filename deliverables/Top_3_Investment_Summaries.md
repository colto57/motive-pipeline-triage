# Top 3 Investment Recommendations

*Selected from the mandate-filtered shortlist produced by the triage tool.*

## 1. Along (Seed · Financial Infrastructure · San Francisco)

### What the company does
Along builds AI agents that automate back-office financial operations for mid-market companies — accounts payable/receivable, bank reconciliation, and month-end close. The buyer is the CFO/finance ops leader at a 200–2,000 employee company drowning in manual workflows across ERP, banks, and spreadsheets.

### What you like
1. **Thesis-native vertical AI.** This is exactly Motive’s “verticalized AI embedded in financial workflows” theme — not generic chat, but agents executing close/reconciliation tasks where error cost is high and software is already budgeted.
2. **Early traction with credible operators.** $600k ARR across 15 paying customers at seed stage, plus ex-Salesforce VP Product and ex-Brex Head of Engineering — a rare combo of enterprise GTM and fintech infrastructure depth.

### Key risks
Mid-market finance teams have long replacement cycles and heavy services onboarding; if implementation requires bespoke integration per customer, gross margin and scaling velocity could look more like a consultancy than a software platform — compressing venture returns.

### First-call questions
1. What percentage of customers go live without professional services, and what is the median time-to-value from signed contract to automated close?
2. Of your 15 customers, how many expanded ARR in the last 90 days — and what workflow (AP, AR, reconciliation) drove expansion?

## 2. Koxa (Series A · Payments / Banking Infrastructure · New York)

### What the company does
Koxa provides banking and payments APIs that let SMBs connect bank accounts, automate payables/receivables, and access working capital. Customers are SMB finance teams and the fintechs/platforms embedding financial workflows for them.

### What you like
1. **Infrastructure wedge with measurable momentum.** $5m ARR with 60% QoQ growth at Series A fits Motive’s banking/payments infrastructure focus — analogous to venture portfolio names like Navro, Parto, and Credix.
2. **Founder-market fit in transaction banking.** Ex-Goldman transaction banking + ex-Plaid engineering is a strong signal for building regulated, high-reliability money movement — the bar Motive cares about for infrastructure bets.

### Key risks
The SMB embedded-finance API space is crowded (Plaid, Stripe Treasury, unit/banking-as-a-service players). If Koxa’s working-capital product competes with its own distribution partners or lacks differentiated underwriting data, net revenue retention could stall even with top-line growth.

### First-call questions
1. What is net dollar retention today, and which product module (payables, receivables, working capital) drives expansion vs. churn?
2. Who owns compliance and sponsor-bank relationships — and what happens to unit economics if interchange/BCA pricing compresses 20%?

## 3. ZeroDrift (Seed · Financial Infrastructure · New York)

### What the company does
ZeroDrift sells real-time financial data reconciliation infrastructure for fintechs and banks, using ML-powered matching to eliminate settlement discrepancies between internal ledgers and external payment/banking rails.

### What you like
1. **Mission-critical pain with institutional validation.** Reconciliation breaks are expensive, regulated, and daily — and two Tier-1 bank design partners suggest the problem is budgeted at the top of the stack, not a “nice-to-have” analytics tool.
2. **Strong adjacency to Motive’s AI/data portfolio.** Closest comps in our model include Synthera AI and Threatfabric — infrastructure + ML applied to operational finance, which maps cleanly to Motive’s “clean data foundations / AI-ready workflows” theme.

### Key risks
Enterprise bank sales cycles are long and pilot-heavy; if design partners remain unpaid evaluations for 12+ months without production contracts, the company may need significantly more capital before ARR catches up to the quality of logos on the website.

### First-call questions
1. For each design partner, what is the contractual path from pilot → paid production — including timeline, decision owner, and budget line item?
2. Where does matching accuracy plateau today, and what fraction of exceptions still require human operators at bank scale (1M+ daily transactions)?
